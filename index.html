The Beginning

Born from a Lifelong Passion

In 2011, Chef Édouard Dax opened the doors of Daxies with a singular dream — to make every guest feel like royalty. Trained under the legendary kitchens of Paris and Tokyo, Édouard fused worlds together, creating a language of food both bold and intimate.

What began as a 30-seat neighbourhood bistro has grown into one of Manhattan's most celebrated dining institutions, earning two Michelin stars in 2016 and numerous James Beard nominations.

"Cooking is not just sustenance. It is memory made tangible — the warmth of a grandmother's kitchen, the thrill of a foreign market, the silence of tasting something extraordinary for the first time."
— Chef Édouard Dax
